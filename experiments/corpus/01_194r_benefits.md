# Section 194R, Income-tax Act 1961 (FY 2024-25) — summary with citations
[PLACEHOLDER SUMMARY — replace with official text before publishing results]

- s.194R(1): any person providing a benefit or perquisite (in cash or kind) arising from
  business or the exercise of a profession to a RESIDENT shall, before providing it, ensure
  tax has been deducted at 10% of the value or aggregate value.
- First proviso: no deduction where the value or aggregate value provided to the resident
  during the financial year does not exceed Rs 20,000. (Once exceeded, deduction applies to
  the aggregate value, not merely the excess.)
- Second proviso: the section does not apply to an individual or HUF provider whose total
  sales/gross receipts/turnover does not exceed Rs 1 crore (business) or Rs 50 lakh
  (profession) in the financial year immediately preceding.
- CBDT Circular 12/2022: a product given to a social-media influencer for promotion is a
  benefit/perquisite IF RETAINED by the influencer; if returned after rendering the service,
  s.194R does not apply. Valuation: fair market value; purchase price if the provider
  purchased it; price charged to customers if manufactured.
- Circular 12/2022 (in-kind benefits): where the benefit is wholly or partly in kind, the
  provider must ensure tax has been PAID before releasing the benefit (recipient's
  advance-tax evidence, or provider bears it).
- Circular 12/2022 Q9 (provider bears the tax): the tax paid by the provider is itself a
  benefit — compute by grossing up: t = v × r / (1 − r).
- s.206AA: where the payee does not furnish PAN, deduct at the higher of the applicable rate
  or 20%.
- Recipient-side note: such benefits are taxable in the recipient's hands u/s 28(iv)
  regardless of whether the provider was required to deduct.
