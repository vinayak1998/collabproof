# Cash-fee TDS: s.194J vs s.194C (FY 2024-25) — summary with citations
[PLACEHOLDER SUMMARY — replace with official text before publishing results]

- s.194J(1): fees for professional services — deduct 10%. Explanation (a): "professional
  services" means services rendered in the course of carrying on legal, medical, engineering,
  architectural, accountancy, technical consultancy, interior decoration or ADVERTISING
  profession, or notified professions. Threshold: no deduction where the aggregate credited/
  paid during the financial year does not exceed Rs 30,000.
- s.194C: payments to contractors for carrying out any "work" — Explanation (iv)(a): "work"
  INCLUDES ADVERTISING. Rate: 1% where the payee is an individual or HUF, 2% otherwise.
  Threshold (s.194C(5)): deduct where a single credit/payment exceeds Rs 30,000, or where
  the aggregate during the financial year exceeds Rs 1,00,000.
- Note the overlap: advertising appears expressly in BOTH sections; classification of a
  direct brand→creator promotional payment is a recognized point of interpretive difficulty
  (cf. CBDT Circular 715 of 1995 on payments to advertising agencies under s.194C).
- s.206AA: no PAN → 20% (both sections).
